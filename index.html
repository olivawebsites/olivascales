The Beginning

My
Story.

Not polished. Not perfect. Real. This is where I came from — and why I'm not stopping.

I didn't grow up with advantages. No trust fund, no connections, no roadmap. My family was humble — the kind of humble where every dollar has a story and you learn early that nothing comes easy. My mother carried us through some of the hardest times imaginable. She worked without complaining, sacrificed without asking for anything back. Watching her is still the reason I don't quit.

Like a lot of people, I tried everything to change my situation. Dropshipping — built stores, ran ads, got a few sales, hit walls. Day trading — stared at charts until midnight, made some wins, gave them back. Crypto — rode the waves, felt the highs, felt the lows. I wasn't failing because I was lazy. I was failing because I hadn't found the thing I was actually built for yet.

Then I came across scaling businesses. And something clicked differently. Not just interest — recognition. Like I had finally found the arena I was supposed to be in. The idea that you could take something that already works and make it grow — that made sense to me in a way nothing else had.

But getting in wasn't easy. I reached out. I pitched. I showed up. And I got rejected — a lot. Business owners didn't know me. Why would they trust me? I had no portfolio, no reputation, no credibility yet. Just hunger. Just belief. Just an obsessive willingness to outwork everyone who had more than me.

"Sometimes the only thing standing between you and everything is one yes."

Then I got one yes. One person decided to bet on me. I treated that opportunity like it was the last door on earth. I over-delivered. I stayed late. I obsessed. That one yes turned into two. Two turned into five. And five is when something inside me finally understood — this is real. This is working. And there's no limit to how far this can go.

I built Olivascales because I know I'm not the only person who's been in that place — trying hard, getting rejected, wondering if it's ever going to happen. It happens. But only if you don't stop before it does.
